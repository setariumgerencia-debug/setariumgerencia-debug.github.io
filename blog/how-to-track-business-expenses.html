How to Track Business Expenses: A Simple System for Small Businesses

Updated May 2026 · 6 min read

Untracked expenses cost you twice: once when you overspend without noticing, and again at tax time when you miss deductions you were entitled to. The good news is you don't need accounting software to fix this. You need a consistent system. Here's one that takes five minutes a week.

Why tracking expenses matters more than you think

Every dollar you can document as a business expense is a dollar you may not pay tax on. Beyond taxes, expense tracking shows you where money actually goes — and it's almost never where owners assume. The small recurring subscriptions, the fees, the "quick" purchases: they add up to real money.

Step 1: Separate business and personal spending

Open a dedicated business account and run every business cost through it. This one habit eliminates the worst part of bookkeeping — untangling which Amazon charge was for the office and which was for groceries. Clean inputs make everything downstream easy.

Step 2: Choose simple, consistent categories

You only need a handful of categories to start: Software, Marketing, Supplies, Travel, Fees, and Contractors. The exact list matters less than using the same names every time. Inconsistent labels are what make expense reports useless.

Step 3: Log expenses as they happen

The biggest mistake is saving it all for "later." Later becomes a painful afternoon of digging through statements. Instead, log each expense the day it happens — date, amount, category, and a one-word note. A spreadsheet open on your phone or laptop is all you need.

Step 4: Total each category automatically

Never add things up by hand. A formula like =SUMIF(category_column, "Software", amount_column) gives you the running total for any category, updated the instant you add a row. Now you can answer "how much am I spending on tools?" in one glance.

Step 5: Review monthly

Once a month, look at the totals and ask two questions: what surprised me, and what can I cut? This 10-minute review is where the savings happen — canceling the tool you forgot about, renegotiating the fee, spotting the category creeping up.

Expense tracking isn't about being perfect. It's about being aware. Separate your accounts, log as you go, let formulas total it up, and review monthly. That's the whole system.
