How to Use ChatGPT for Marketing Your Small Business

Updated May 2026 · 8 min read

Marketing eats time most small business owners don't have. ChatGPT won't replace your strategy or your voice, but it can take the heavy lifting out of the repetitive parts — brainstorming, drafting, and reformatting. Here's how to put it to work across your marketing, with prompts you can adapt today.

1. Generate content ideas you'd never run dry on

Stuck on what to post? Give it context and ask for volume. Prompt: "I run a [type of business] serving [audience]. Give me 20 content ideas that answer questions my customers actually ask. For each, suggest the format — post, video, or email." Now you have a month of ideas in seconds.

2. Draft social posts in your voice

The trick is showing it your style first. Paste one or two of your best past posts and say: "Match this tone. Write 5 posts promoting [offer], each with a hook and a call to action." Feeding it examples beats describing your voice in words.

3. Write and improve email campaigns

Prompt: "Write a 3-email sequence for new subscribers who downloaded my [freebie]. Email 1 welcomes and delivers value, email 2 builds trust with a story, email 3 makes a soft offer. Keep each under 150 words." Then refine: "Make email 3 less salesy."

4. Turn one piece of content into ten

Repurposing is where AI saves the most time. "Take this blog post and turn it into: 5 social captions, 3 email subject lines, and 1 short script for a video. Keep my casual tone. [paste]" One article becomes a week of content.

5. Sharpen your ad copy

Ask for variety, then pick: "Write 8 headline options and 4 short ad descriptions for [product], aimed at [audience]. Make half benefit-focused and half curiosity-focused." Testing several angles is how good ads are found.

The one rule that separates good output from generic

Always give context: who you are, who you're talking to, and what a good result looks like. "Write a post" gets you a generic post. "Write a post for busy freelancers about saving time on invoicing, casual tone, one CTA" gets you something you can actually use. Treat the first answer as a draft and refine from there.
