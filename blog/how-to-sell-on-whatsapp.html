How to Sell on WhatsApp: A Beginner's Guide for Small Business

Updated May 2026 · 7 min read

For millions of small businesses, WhatsApp is the storefront, the sales desk, and the support line all at once. But selling well on chat is a skill, not just having the app installed. Get the setup and the conversation flow right and WhatsApp becomes one of your highest-converting channels. Here's how to start.

Step 1: Use WhatsApp Business, not regular WhatsApp

The free WhatsApp Business app gives you tools the regular app doesn't: a business profile with your hours and catalog, quick replies for common questions, automated greeting and away messages, and labels to organize chats by stage. It's the foundation — set it up before anything else.

Step 2: Make it easy to start a conversation

Add a "click to chat" link (wa.me/yourNumber) to your bio, website, and posts so customers reach you in one tap. The easier you are to message, the more conversations you start — and conversations are where sales happen.

Step 3: Open with relevance, not a pitch

When someone messages you, resist the urge to dump your whole offer. Ask a question that helps you understand what they need: "Happy to help — what are you trying to solve?" People buy when they feel understood, not sold to.

Step 4: Build trust fast

On chat, trust is built through speed and clarity. Reply quickly, answer plainly, and don't dodge the price. Sharing a quick photo, a short voice note, or a customer result can do more than a paragraph of text.

Step 5: Close with a tiny next step

Big asks stall a chat; small asks move it forward. Instead of "Do you want to buy?", try "Want me to send the link so you can lock it in?" The easier the next step, the more often people take it.

Step 6: Follow up — politely and consistently

Most sales need more than one message. If someone goes quiet, a friendly check-in a day or two later recovers deals you'd otherwise lose: "Hey, still keen to sort this out? Happy to answer anything." Persistence done politely is what separates closers from order-takers.

Selling on WhatsApp comes down to being easy to reach, genuinely helpful, fast to reply, and clear about the next step. Set up the Business app, lead with questions, and always follow up.
