15 ChatGPT Prompts Every Small Business Owner Should Use

Updated May 2026 · 8 min read

Most people get mediocre answers from ChatGPT or Claude because they ask vague questions. The fix isn't a better AI — it's a better prompt. A good business prompt gives the model a role, context, and a clear output format. Below are practical prompts you can adapt today, plus the formula behind them.

The anatomy of a prompt that works

Weak prompt: "Write a marketing email." Strong prompt: "You are an email copywriter. Write a 120-word promotional email for a $14 finance template aimed at freelancers. Friendly tone, one clear call to action, subject line included." The second one specifies role, length, audience, tone, and format — so the answer is usable, not generic.

Sales & outreach prompts

  • Cold email: "Act as a B2B sales writer. Write a short cold email to a [type of business] owner offering [your service]. Lead with a problem they feel, keep it under 100 words, end with a low-pressure question."
  • Follow-up: "Write three follow-up messages for a prospect who opened my proposal but didn't reply. Space them politely over two weeks. Vary the angle each time."
  • Objection handling: "A customer says my price is too high. Give me three honest, non-pushy responses that reframe value instead of discounting."

Marketing & content prompts

  • Content calendar: "Create a 2-week social post calendar for a [business type]. For each day give a hook, the post idea, and a CTA. Mix education, story, and promotion."
  • Repurpose: "Turn this blog post into 5 short social captions and 3 email subject lines. Keep my casual tone. [paste text]"
  • Headlines: "Give me 10 headline options for an article about [topic]. Make half curiosity-driven and half benefit-driven."

Operations & strategy prompts

  • SOP draft: "Write a step-by-step standard operating procedure for [task] so a new hire could follow it without asking questions."
  • Decision helper: "I'm deciding between [A] and [B] for my business. List the trade-offs in a table, then give your recommendation and the one risk I'm probably underestimating."
  • Pricing sanity check: "Here's my offer and price. Play a skeptical customer and tell me the three objections you'd raise before buying."

Three rules to get better answers

  1. Give context. Tell the AI who you are, who the audience is, and what "good" looks like.
  2. Ask for a format. "In a table," "in 5 bullets," "under 100 words" — constraints produce sharper output.
  3. Iterate. The first answer is a draft. Reply with "make it shorter / more specific / less salesy" and it improves fast.

AI won't replace your judgment, but the right prompt turns it into a tireless assistant for the boring 80% of business writing. Steal the templates above and adapt them to your voice.
